Scroll-driven operating picture

Fabio turns an opening range into a controlled options workflow.

This story walks through the June 5 dashboard snapshot: how the bot moves from regime filters, to entries, to circuit discipline, to end-of-day flattening. Scrolling changes the evidence, not the browser's native behavior.

Scene 1 / System contract

Every session starts with a gate, not a trade.

Fabio watches SPY, QQQ, and NVDA, but the first decision is whether the market context is eligible. The visual starts with the full session ledger and highlights trade days versus blocked days.

  • Visual layer: daily P&L bars, cumulative equity, and no-trade reason cells.
  • Static fallback: full month ledger with trade and block days visible.
Scene 2 / Opening range

The opening range compresses the morning into one decision band.

Current research and live logic define the opening range from 09:30 to 09:44 ET on 5-minute bars. Breakouts still need trend and regime agreement before risk is committed.

  • Visual change: timeline narrows to the first 15 minutes, then marks the confirmation window.
  • Why scroll helps: the reader sees the gate sequence in operational order.
Scene 3 / Early acceleration

May 5-8 created the first equity step-up.

The first winning cluster carried the dashboard out of its opening loss. The equity line moves from negative territory into a sustained positive run over four sessions.

  • Evidence: +$2,407, +$2,781, +$2,332, and +$2,185 in consecutive sessions.
  • Static fallback: highlighted bars and cumulative line at May 8.
Scene 4 / Drawdown control

The mid-May drawdown is visible, but it is bounded.

May 11-18 produced a losing stretch. The visual dims wins and focuses on the loss cluster so it is clear where performance was paid back.

  • Evidence: five negative trading sessions in the cluster, bottoming at May 18.
  • Risk note: this is paper trading research, not a forecast.
Scene 5 / No-trade discipline

No trade is an explicit outcome.

Several days show no closed positions because the bot was off, late, below the VIX floor, blocked by red-day filters, or rejected counter-trend signals. The reason matrix moves from P&L to control state.

  • Evidence: May 19, May 26-29, June 1, June 3, and June 5 record no-trade reasons.
  • Browser behavior: native scroll, no wheel interception, keyboard scroll remains standard.
Scene 6 / June reset

June 4 shows the cleanest recent closed session.

After multiple block days, June 4 closed two winning positions for +$5,118. The story shifts from filter state back to trade outcome and end-of-day completion.

  • Evidence: two winners, zero losers, and the largest single-day net P&L in the snapshot.
  • Static fallback: final equity line and the June 4 bar are both labeled.
Scene 7 / Live operations

The repo supports a narrow live pipeline.

The operational path is deliberately simple: data and regime checks feed the strategy loop, orders route through OpenD, the dashboard records session state, and the fail-safe can flatten leftovers after the primary close.

  • Implementation stack: static HTML, SVG, IntersectionObserver, and native sticky layout.
  • Reduced motion: all scene frames remain stacked with the same labels and data claims.
